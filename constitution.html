Babson Technology Society Constitution and By-Laws

ARTICLE I: NAME & PURPOSE

Section A: Name

The name of this club shall be the Babson Technology Society.

Section B: Mission Statement

To teach more and more people how to practically apply the concepts formulated by our society on topics concerning new technology.

Section C: Purpose

  1. Discuss new concepts theorized by leaders in the field and dissecting the applications of the theory to real life.
  2. To give people the opportunity to learn how to apply the technology of today in real-life circumstances.
  3. Give a place for people to learn, teach, and apply various aspects of technology.
  4. Allow people to see why they should learn aspects of current technology.
  5. Give people to explore their ideas with current technology through the group.

ARTICLE II: MEMBERSHIP & DUES

Section A: Eligibility

Membership shall be open to anyone interested in the concept of new technology. They also must maintain a GPA at Babson College of at least 2.5 (≥ 2.5).

Section B: Dues

There shall be no dues to join the club; however, if the club chooses to enter a competition, appropriate funds may be requested from members within the club. Bringing money in to play around with will not be accepted; members will not be allowed to gamble within any club meetings.

ARTICLE III: OFFICERS

Section A: Officers

The officer roles shall consist of the President, Vice-President, Director of Operations, Director of Programming, and the Treasurer.

Section B: Eligibility

Officers must be students of Babson. They also must maintain a GPA at Babson College of at least 2.5 (≥ 2.5).

Section C: Election

The President shall be voted by the consensus of the current officers. The rest of the officers shall be elected by 4/17 from a majority vote of the club membership.

Section D: Term

The officers shall serve for one year and their term of office shall begin at the conclusion of the spring semester.

Section E: Vacancy

If a vacancy occurs in the office of President, the Vice-President shall assume the office for the remainder of the term, and vacancies in any other office shall be filled by a special election.

ARTICLE IV: DUTIES OF OFFICERS

Section A: President

  • Preside at meetings.
  • Vote only in case of a tie.
  • Represent the club.
  • Appoint committee chairpersons subject to the approval of the Executive Board.
  • Help major club decision in deciding how the lessons should run and which competitions.
  • Maintain the club vision of learning more of advancements and finding ways to allow those in the club to practically go about doing business by making the advancements more accessible.

ARTICLE V: MEETINGS

Section A: Meetings

Regular meetings shall be held bi-weekly during the regular school year.

Section B: Special Meeting

Special meetings may be called by the President with the approval of the Executive Board.

Section C: Quorum

A quorum shall consist of two-thirds (2/3) of the membership.

ARTICLE VI: EXECUTIVE BOARD

Section A: Responsibility

Management of this club shall be vested in an Executive Board responsible to the entire membership to uphold these bylaws.

Section B: Membership

This board shall consist of the officers as listed in Article III.

Section C: Meetings

This board shall meet at least once before every regular meeting of the club to organize and plan future activities.

ARTICLE VII: ADVISOR

Section A: Selection

There shall be a faculty/staff advisor who shall be selected each year by the membership.

Section B: Duties

  • Maintain an awareness of the activities and programs sponsored by the student club.
  • Meet on a regular basis with the leader of the student club to discuss upcoming meetings, long-range plans, goals, and problems of the club.

ARTICLE VIII: COMMITTEES

Section A: Committees

  • Committee of Scholarship - focused on learning about more complex topics in current technology and clarifying difficult concepts to members.
  • Committee of Competition - focused on winning competitions related to game theory.

Section B: Special Committees

The President shall have the authority to appoint any special committees with the approval of the Executive Board from time to time as need demands.

ARTICLE IX: AMENDMENTS

Section A: Selection

These bylaws may be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of the membership.

Section B: Notice

All members shall receive advance notice of the proposed amendment at least five days before the meeting.
